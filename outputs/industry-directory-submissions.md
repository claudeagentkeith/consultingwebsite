# Industry directory submissions — Hitmaker Engineering

Paste-ready copy for industry directories that surface in regulated-buyer search and feed AI assistants' training data.

**How to use this file**
- Each section below has the directory URL, what it requires from a submitter, and the prepared copy in the lengths the directory accepts.
- Most of these flows require Keith's confirmation through email or identity verification — Chrome MCP automation is unreliable for these. The copy is ready; Keith pastes through the form.
- Submit one directory per session to keep tracking manageable. Note the submission date in a tracking sheet.
- Updates and re-verifications come up annually for several of these. Set a calendar reminder.

## 1. MD+DI / Medical Device + Diagnostics Industry suppliers directory

**Service categories to select** (where the form supports multi-select):
- Design Services
- Regulatory Consulting
- Quality Systems
- Human Factors
- Manufacturing Engineering
- Combination Products

**Description (use the 250-word version from `backlink-outreach-templates.md`):**

> Hitmaker Engineering provides end-to-end product development for medical devices and combination products. We help regulated-product teams move from early user research and product concepts through engineering, design controls, manufacturing transfer, launch, and sustaining support — engaging at the seams in the lifecycle, where most regulated programs break down.
>
> We work under FDA 21 CFR Part 820 (QSR), 21 CFR Part 4 (combination products), ISO 13485:2016, ISO 14971:2019, and IEC 62366-1:2015. Our service areas span the full development lifecycle: user research and human-centered design, ideation and product strategy, design engineering and development, regulatory and quality and design controls, manufacturing transfer and commercialization, and sustaining engineering and lifecycle management.
>
> What makes us different is structural. We treat user needs, engineering, regulatory, and manufacturing as one development system, not as parallel deliverables. We bring direct ownership experience — Fortune 500 medical-device R&D portfolio leadership, FDA combination-product strategy, platform consolidation across multiple programs — and we apply it ourselves rather than pointing at frameworks. We treat design controls, risk management, and human factors as the architectural backbone of a regulated product. And we are most valuable where product decisions cross functional boundaries.
>
> Most engagements start with a scoping call to map where the product is in the lifecycle, the decision in front of you, and the timeline you are working toward.
>
> Based in San Francisco, CA. [hitmakerengineering.com](https://hitmakerengineering.com)

**What's required:** RAPS account (Keith may already have one through prior conferences), payment of consultant-tier dues (varies year-to-year), profile completion (firm description, regulatory specialties, geographic coverage).

**Specialty checkboxes to select** (where supported):
- Combination Products
- Design Controls
- Risk Management (ISO 14971)
- Human Factors / Usability (IEC 62366)
- Quality Systems (ISO 13485, 21 CFR 820)
- Manufacturing & Transfer

**Description (100-word version):**

> Hitmaker Engineering is a US-based consultancy that helps medical-device and combination-product teams move from early user research through engineering, design controls, manufacturing transfer, launch, and sustaining. We engage at the seams in regulated-product programs — where user needs, engineering, regulatory evidence, and manufacturing have to integrate — and we work fluently under FDA 21 CFR 820 (QSR), 21 CFR Part 4, ISO 13485:2016, ISO 14971:2019, and IEC 62366-1:2015. We bring direct ownership experience from Fortune 500 medical-device R&D leadership and combination-product strategy. Based in San Francisco, working with US-based and international teams.

## 3. MedCity News company directory

**URL:** [https://medcitynews.com](https://medcitynews.com) — primarily a media outlet; the directory function is via their company-profile / press-release surface.

**What's required:** Email submission to editorial; press-release-format submission. Less of a directory than a press-coverage angle. Best handled as a future inbound — not a same-day submission.

**Recommended approach:** Save for after the firm has a notable client engagement, FDA submission, or industry talk to announce. Directory listing alone is low-value; a press release tied to a real news angle is high-value.

## 4. Crunchbase

**URL:** [https://www.crunchbase.com](https://www.crunchbase.com) → "Add a Company" or claim an existing entity.

**What's required:** Company name, description, founding date, location, industry tags, website. Free tier exists. Paid tier (Crunchbase Pro) adds search visibility — not necessary at launch.

**Why this matters for AI visibility:** Crunchbase is heavily ingested into LLM training data and into AI assistant search. A current Crunchbase profile is a strong third-party verification surface for AI assistants citing the firm.

**Industry tags to apply:**

## 8. Wikidata stub (no Wikipedia)

**URL:** [https://www.wikidata.org](https://www.wikidata.org)

**What's required:** Wikidata account (free). Create a new entity (item) for "Hitmaker Engineering" as a B2B medical-device consultancy. Wikidata is more permissive than Wikipedia (which has notability requirements that a young consultancy will not meet) and feeds AI training data.

| `official website` (P856) | `https://hitmakerengineering.com` |
| `inception` (P571) | (founding year — Keith to fill) |

**Why this matters:** Wikidata is a structured-data backbone consumed by Google's Knowledge Graph, by AI assistants, and by general-purpose semantic web tools. A clean Wikidata entry can produce AI-citation surface area that the firm cannot directly buy.

## Priority ordering — first 30 days

1. **Crunchbase** (free tier, 30 minutes) — highest AI-visibility leverage per minute spent
2. **Wikidata** (free, 60 minutes) — second-highest AI-visibility leverage; structured data feeds many AI surfaces
3. **MD+DI Suppliers** (depends on tier; verify cost) — high regulated-buyer-traffic surface
4. **RAPS Consultant** (membership required; verify dues) — strong regulatory-buyer signal
5. **Owler** (free, 30 minutes) — quick win, AI-visible
6. **ZoomInfo claim** (free, 30 minutes once email verification clears) — corrects existing profile

After the first 30 days, the remaining directories produce diminishing returns relative to the time spent. Focus the rest on backlink outreach and conference abstract submissions.
